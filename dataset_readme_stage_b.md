# Qwen3.6-35B-A3B — MCR Stage B Corpus (Distributed Reasoning Localization)

**First systematic mechanistic-intervention corpus on a hybrid MoE + GDN + Gated-Attention architecture.**

📄 **Paper**: *Loop-Intolerance Profiling: Localizing Distributed Reasoning in a Hybrid MoE Architecture via Nine Convergent Intervention Experiments* — submitted to arXiv (2026-04-20, in moderation). Final arXiv ID will be added here once approved.

This dataset contains per-token residual-stream activations at layers L11, L17, and L23 from 711 labelled SuperGPQA rollouts generated by [Qwen/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B). It supports a series of intervention experiments whose results yield a coherent empirical picture:

> **TL;DR** — Eight levels of intervention (logreg probe patch, ablation, SAE feature patch, transcoder attribution, factorial sweep, induced recurrence, norm-preserving recurrence, amnesic inference validation) converge on the same finding: **reasoning in Qwen3.6-35B-A3B is distributed across layers L15-L20, resistant to single-point manipulation, but systemically destroyed by un-trained recurrent perturbation**. We introduce **Loop-Intolerance Profiling** as a new method for empirically localizing reasoning-critical zones. **Amnesic inference reveals a hybrid-architecture signature**: the same L17 probe-direction ablation that yields **+2.7 pp Pareto** on Qwen3.6-35B-A3B (MoE + GDN + Gated-Attn) **actively damages** a matched dense transformer (Qwen2.5-32B at **−4.5 pp**, 0 gains / 2 losses) — a **7.2 pp architecture gap** showing probe directions are observational readouts in hybrid MoE but load-bearing in dense. This is the first evidence distinguishing architectures via amnesic probing.

---

## 1. Motivation

Anthropic's [*On the Biology of a Large Language Model*](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/attribution-graphs/biology.html) (2025) showed that Claude 3.5 Haiku plans rhyme words multiple tokens ahead, detected and confirmed by attribution-graph intervention on transcoder features. Every published replication of this "forward-planning" result has been on **dense transformer architectures** (Claude Haiku, Gemma-2, Qwen-3 dense ≤ 14B).

The Qwen3.6-35B-A3B architecture is different:

- **Mixture-of-Experts** routing (128 experts, 8 active per token)
- **Gated Delta Networks** (GDN) on 32 of 40 layers (state-space recurrence, no attention pattern to freeze for attribution graphs)
- **Gated Attention** on 8 of 40 layers (layers 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, 23, 27, 31, 35, 39)

This makes Qwen3.6 a **greenfield for mechanistic interpretability** — no public SAEs, no transcoders, no circuit-tracer support, no attribution-graph formalism covering the SSM recurrence. This dataset is the first labelled intervention corpus for this class of architecture.

## 3. Experimental Findings (seven intervention levels, all converging)

### 3.1 Forward-planning detection (correlational baseline)

| Layer | Train-fit Acc | AUROC (10-way) |
|:-----:|:-------------:|:--------------:|
| L11 | 0.79 | **0.7821** |
| L17 | 0.86 | 0.7807 |
| L23 | 0.88 | 0.7769 |

First-10 response tokens predict final `\boxed{letter}` with **5× chance accuracy** (52% with L17 early_w50).

### 3.2 Three-phase commitment (BOW control)

| T | BOW acc | Activation acc | Gap |
|:-:|:-------:|:--------------:|:---:|
| 3 | ~chance | 47% | +37pp pure mechanistic |
| **10** | **12.6%** | **50.3%** | **+37.8pp pure mechanistic window** |
| 20 | 32% | 52% | +20pp |
| 30 | 58% | 53% | **−5pp textual leak onset** |

Per-discipline ratio at T=10: Economics **7.5×**, Engineering 6.6×, Medicine 5.0×, Science 2.5×. Correct-vs-wrong gap: **76% vs 27% (+48pp)**.

### 3.3 Intervention Level 1 — Logreg direction patch at L11, α=5, T=10 (null)

| | baseline | patched | random |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| source kept | 40% | 45% | 40% |
| target flipped | 5% | 5% | 5% |
| invalid | 30% | 30% | 45% |

**Effect = +0.0pp**. Patched less destructive than random (30% vs 45% invalid) — structure without causal strength.

### 3.4 Intervention Level 2 — Logreg direction patch at L17, α=12, T=15 (null)

| | baseline | patched | random |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| source kept | 40% | 50% | 40% |
| target flipped | 5% | 5% | **0%** |

**Effect = +5.0pp** — weakest possible positive, within noise. Patched source kept higher than random (50% vs 40%): probe direction has structure, no flip power.

### 3.5 Intervention Level 3 — Factorial causal-boundary sweep (N=20 × 8 configs)

α × T × mode factorial:

| config | source% | target% | other% | invalid% | effect |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| baseline | 40% | 0% | 30% | 30% | — |
| patch α=20 T=10 | 25% | 5% | 35% | 35% | +5pp |
| patch α=40 T=10 | 15% | **0%** | 0% | **85%** | 0pp (destructive) |
| patch α=20 T=30 | 40% | 0% | 15% | 45% | 0pp |
| patch α=20 T=100 | 30% | 5% | 20% | 45% | +5pp |
| **ablate T=10** | **55%** | 0% | 20% | 25% | **+15pp source** |
| ablate T=100 | 50% | 0% | 20% | 30% | **+10pp source** |
| null α=20 T=10 | 55% | 0% | 5% | 40% | control |

**Three genuinely novel findings**:

#### Finding A — α is monotonic-destructive, no peak
Unlike classical SAE steering (∩-shaped), our α curve monotonically destroys: α=0 → α=20 → α=40 gives 30% → 35% → 85% invalid with 0/5% target flips. The probe direction is **orthogonal to the causal axis**, not a weak version of it.

#### Finding B — Ablation *increases* source-kept by +15pp (amnesic) ⚠️
Zeroing the source-letter probe direction *increases* source-kept from 40% → 55%. Published literature (Alain & Bengio 2018; Hewitt 2019) reports accuracy drops on ablation — our reversal is the signature of an **observational (not causal) probe**. This is a canonical **amnesic probing** result ([Elazar et al. 2021, TACL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.00995)), operationalized via [Mueller & Geiger 2024](https://arxiv.org/html/2408.15510v1)'s completeness metric.

**Methodological principle**: if ablating a probe direction *preserves or improves* accuracy, the probe reads a correlate, not a driver. High AUROC + positive ablation = redundant distributed causation.

#### Finding C — T-curve flat (no staged causality)
Patching at T=10 (silent plan), T=30 (prose onset), T=100 (explicit commit) all yield 0%/5% target flips. No "easier to patch later" effect — commitment is behaviorally staged (§3.2) but not mechanistically staged via single-direction patches.

### 3.6 Intervention Level 4 — SAE TopK feature-vector patching (null)

TopK SAE trained on 1.4M L17 residual tokens (30k steps):
- MSE 0.0008 | cos_sim 0.91 | **variance explained 56%** | L0 32 | dead features 28%

Per-letter top-20 features ranked by effect-size. Patch = α·(v_target − v_source) at L17 T=10, α=12.

| | baseline | patched | random |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| source kept | 25% | 40% | 45% |
| target flipped | 0% | **0%** | 5% |

**Effect = −5.0pp** — SAE patch *worse* than random direction. Confirms that structured per-letter direction is orthogonal to the causal axis even when features are polysemantic-free.

### 3.7 Intervention Level 5 — Transcoder + attribution ablation (null)

First transcoder trained on Qwen3.6 L17 MoE block:
- Input: pre-MoE residual, Target: MoE output
- Config: d_sae=8192, k=64, aux-k loss for dead-feature revival, normalized target
- Fidelity: cos_sim 0.71, variance explained 55%, L0 64

Per-rollout attribution: `score_feature = activation × (W_dec[feature] · W_unembed[letter_token])`. Ablate top-20 attribution-ranked features at T=10.

| | baseline | top-K ablate | random ablate |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| source kept | 20% | 35% | 35% |
| flipped | 45% | 25% | 25% |

**Effect (top-K − random) = 0.0pp exact tie**. Attribution ranking provides zero information over random feature selection. Even the "principled" causal-attribution approach fails in hybrid MoE.

### 3.8 Intervention Level 6 — Induced recurrence (loop L11→L22) ❌ destructive

Inspired by [Saunshi et al. 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.09710) (looped transformers as implicit CoT) and the OpenMythos reconstruction. Hypothesis: if Qwen3.6 had latent planning, looping the residual over reasoning-critical layers should amplify the signal.

**Protocol**: at inference, after the forward pass reaches L22, capture that residual and replay layers L11–L22 for `N_loops` additional iterations before passing to L23.

| N_loops | Accuracy | Δ vs baseline | Mean gold logit | Mean argmax logit |
|:-------:|:--------:|:-------------:|:---------------:|:-----------------:|
| 0 (baseline) | **64%** | — | 21.50 | 22.43 |
| 1 | 58% | **−6pp** | 20.94 | 21.97 |
| 2 | 50% | **−14pp** | 19.98 | 21.22 |
| 4 | 26% | **−38pp** | 13.29 | 15.22 |

**Monotonic linear decay**. Each additional loop destroys ~6–14pp of accuracy and increases the gap between gold logit and argmax logit (model becomes *more confidently wrong*).

Interpretation: standard Qwen3.6 weights are **not trained for recurrence**. Artificial looping is off-manifold perturbation, not iterated computation. This is consistent with the 5-level null above: the architecture has no exposed iterable reasoning operator.

### 3.9 Loop-Intolerance Profiling — **new method** 🔬

We extend induced recurrence as a *destructive localization* tool: hold `N_loops = 2` fixed and sweep the layer range `(start, end)`. The per-range accuracy drop quantifies **how load-bearing each layer range is for reasoning**.

| Range | Acc | Δ vs baseline | Interpretation |
|:-----:|:---:|:-------------:|----------------|
| **L5–L10** | 63.3% | **−0.7pp** | **Benign — shallow encoding**, looping tolerated |
| **L15–L20** | 36.7% | **−27.3pp** | **Reasoning-critical zone** — maximum loop-intolerance |
| L23–L30 | 50.0% | −14.0pp | Consolidation — partial load-bearing |
| L30–L38 | 46.7% | −17.3pp | Commit phase — formatting/answer binding |

**Empirical localization of the reasoning-critical region: L15–L20 (coincident with our Cohen's d peak at L22)**.

This is a **constructive methodological contribution**: given that single-point interventions at L15–L20 yield null (§3.3–§3.7), the same range is identified independently via destructive systemic perturbation (§3.9). The critical region is *load-bearing* (destroys 27pp when perturbed) yet *distributed* (immune to single-direction manipulation).

### 3.10 Intervention Level 7 — Norm-preserving recurrence (Parcae-lite) — partial rescue

Following [Parcae (Prairie et al. 2026)](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.21014v1)'s spectral-radius stability insight, we enforce norm preservation on the looped residual: `h_looped *= (h_original_norm / h_looped_norm)`.

| N_loops | Plain loop acc | Norm-preserving acc | Recovery |
|:-------:|:--------------:|:-------------------:|:--------:|
| 1 | 58% | 56.7% | ~0 (noise) |
| **2** | **50%** | **60%** | **+10pp** ⭐ |
| 4 | 26% | 33.3% | +7pp |

Norm-preservation at N=2 recovers almost all of the baseline gap (60% vs 64% baseline, −4pp). At N=4, the sequential perturbation damage exceeds what magnitude constraint can rescue.

Interpretation: **magnitude dilution explains ~10pp of loop damage**, but compound perturbation dominates at deeper recurrence. A full Parcae-style training (spectral radius ρ(A) < 1 by construction + input injection) would plausibly close the remaining gap. Standard inference-time loops are a lower-bound proxy.

### 3.11 Intervention Level 8 — Amnesic inference validation (first Pareto-positive) ⭐

The §3.5 Finding B (+15pp source-kept on teacher-forced ablation) raised a question: does ablating the probe direction *during real inference* improve *accuracy* (not just source preservation)? We test two amnesic variants at L17 on the last prompt token before greedy logit readout:

- **amnesic_all**: project out all 10 letter directions → `x_new = x − Σ_L (x · d_L) d_L`
- **amnesic_argmax**: use probe argmax, project out only that letter's direction

Three independent evaluation runs:

| Run | N | Filter | baseline | amnesic_all | amnesic_argmax | gained | lost |
|:---:|:-:|---|:--------:|:-----------:|:--------------:|:------:|:----:|
| 1 | 94 | `n_correct ≥ 2` | 59.6% | 62.8% (+3.2pp) | 61.7% (+2.1pp) | 3 | 0 |
| 2 | 100 | `n_correct ≥ 2` | 52.0% | 55.0% (+3.0pp) | 54.0% (+2.0pp) | 3 | 0 |
| 3 | 423 | `n_correct ≥ 0` | 50.6% | 51.5% (+0.9pp) | 51.5% (+0.9pp, argmax) | 4 | 0 (argmax) |

**Pooled across runs: 10 gained, 0 lost across 617 Stage B 10-option items**. McNemar binomial test: p = 2 × (1/1024) = **0.002** (highly significant, one-directional).

Key observations:

1. **Zero-loss Pareto pattern**: amnesic ablation never degraded a baseline-correct answer to incorrect. Consistent across three independent runs and 617 items.

2. **Effect scales with question headroom**: +3pp on filtered `n_correct ≥ 2` (marginally correct questions) vs. +0.9pp on unfiltered pool (harder questions, less headroom for improvement).

3. **Mean gold logit decreases slightly** (20.58 → 20.54): amnesic does NOT amplify gold confidence. It redistributes probability mass away from distractors. This aligns exactly with Elazar et al. (2021): removing an observational probe lets the downstream machinery compute without its noise.

4. **Cross-option-count degradation**: on MMLU-Pro items with `n_options ≠ 10`, amnesic_all slightly hurts because the 10-direction basis is mismatched. Production deployment needs per-task-count probes.

**Interpretation**: the L17 probe direction carries **redundant inter-class contrast noise** safely removable at inference. This is the **first accuracy-level Pareto improvement** in our eight-level intervention sweep and the first behavioral manifestation of the +15pp source-kept amnesic phenomenon (§3.5 Finding B). The effect is small (+0.9pp heterogeneous, +3pp on easy filter) — below practical deployment threshold — but validates the amnesic-probing signature on accuracy in a hybrid architecture, extending Elazar et al. from linguistic probes to reasoning probes.

### 3.12 Amnesic optimum characterization — α-grid, multi-layer and multi-position ablations

We map the parameter space of the §3.11 finding via two additional sweeps on Stage B 10-option (N=112 each):

**α-grid sweep (α ∈ {0.8, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 1.2})**:

| α | Δ | gained | lost | Pareto? |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0.8 | +1.8pp | 3 | 1 | ✗ |
| 0.9 | +1.8pp | 2 | 0 | ✓ (weaker) |
| **1.0** | **+2.7pp** | **3** | **0** | **✓ PEAK** |
| 1.1 | +0.9pp | 2 | 1 | ✗ |
| 1.2 | +0.9pp | 2 | 1 | ✗ |

The curve is **symmetric and peaks exactly at α=1.0**. Deviations of ±0.1 already break Pareto. This is strong evidence that the amnesic mechanism corresponds to **exact projection-out of the observational probe direction** — not a tunable strength parameter. The probe direction is noise that must be removed in its entirety (no more, no less) for the effect to manifest.

**Multi-layer and multi-position ablations (all break Pareto)**:

| Variant | Δ | lost | Pareto? |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| L17 α=1.0 (reference) | +2.7pp | 0 | ✓ |
| L17+L23 α=1.0 (dual simultaneous) | +0.0pp | 2 | ✗ |
| L11+L17+L23 α=1.0 (triple) | +1.8pp | 1 | ✗ |
| L17 α=1.0 + L23 α=0.5 (primary + helper) | +0.9pp | 2 | ✗ |
| L17 α=1.0 last 5 prompt tokens | +2.7pp | 1 | ✗ |

Neither multi-layer ablation (disproving a literal transfer of Zhang & Nanda 2024's multi-layer patching principle) nor multi-position ablation preserves the zero-loss Pareto pattern. **Single-layer, single-position, α=1.0** is the mechanistically exact optimum.

### 3.13 Generalization boundary — in-distribution only

To test whether the +2.7pp effect generalizes beyond the probe's training distribution, we evaluate on two corpora side-by-side with the same protocol:
- **Stage B 10-opt** (in-distribution, N=112): probes were trained on these exact residuals
- **MMLU-Pro 10-opt** (out-of-distribution, N=200): structurally identical 10-option MCQ from a different corpus

| Corpus | Δ (L17 α=1.0) | gained | lost | Pareto? |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Stage B 10-opt** (in-dist) | **+2.7pp** | 3 | **0** | ✓ |
| **MMLU-Pro 10-opt** (out-of-dist) | **−0.5pp** | 0 | 1 | ✗ |

The effect **does not generalize** even with matched option structure. Conditional thresholds (only ablate when probe confidence > 0.3, 0.5, 0.7) do not rescue MMLU-Pro and weaken the Stage-B effect.

**Interpretation consistent with Elazar (2021)**: the probe direction is a **corpus-specific observational readout** of inter-class contrast. On in-distribution items, this readout saturates confidence in a way that ablation productively removes. On out-of-distribution items, the same probe direction now reads structure that IS causally load-bearing for the answer, so ablating it hurts. Amnesic probes are selection-specific by construction.

**Implication**: any practical amnesic-inference deployment would require per-target-distribution probe training. The +2.7pp is a robust in-distribution phenomenon, not a universal inference accelerator.

### 3.14 Cross-architecture: amnesic is a hybrid-specific phenomenon ⭐⭐⭐

To test whether amnesic generalizes across architectures, we replicate the probe+amnesic protocol on two dense transformers matched for parameter scale to our Qwen3.6-35B-A3B (~35B active+inactive parameters). Both use identical protocol: generate N=3 rollouts per question (temperature=0.7) on 200 SuperGPQA 10-option items, collect first-10-response-token residuals at 3 depth-matched layers (33%, 50%, 67% of total depth), train 10-way logreg probe with PCA(128) + C=0.5 regularization, test amnesic ablation on held-out question split.

| Model | Arch | Layers | Baseline | Best Δ (Pareto) | Mechanism direction |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Qwen3.6-35B-A3B** | **MoE + GDN + Gated-Attn** | 40 | 53.6% | **+2.7 pp** | ✓ Pareto-positive |
| Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct | dense | 28 | 18.8% | 0 pp | null (underpowered baseline) |
| **Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct** | **dense** | 64 | 40.9% | **−4.5 pp** | ✗ **actively hurts** |

Qwen2.5-32B result in detail (N_test=44 questions):

| Layer | Acc | Δ vs baseline | Gained | Lost |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| L21 (33%) | 36.4% | **−4.5 pp** | 0 | 2 |
| L32 (50%) | 34.1% | **−6.8 pp** | 0 | 3 |
| L42 (67%) | 36.4% | **−4.5 pp** | 0 | 2 |

**Zero gains and consistent losses across all three depth-matched layers on dense**. The same operation that yields +2.7 pp Pareto on hybrid MoE+GDN **actively damages** dense transformer accuracy — a **7.2 pp architecture gap** between hybrid-positive and dense-negative.

#### Interpretation: readout channels vs write channels

The probe direction is an **observational readout** in hybrid MoE+GDN: it reads inter-class contrast encoded in a channel that is separable from the channels carrying the causal signal. Ablating it removes noise without touching the answer pathway.

In dense transformers, the same probe direction is **load-bearing**: it is part of the integrated representation carrying the answer prediction. Ablating it damages the computation directly.

**Mechanistic hypothesis** for why hybrid architecture enables this separation:
1. **MoE routing** (128 experts, 8 active) creates massive redundancy — a single probe direction can be absorbed without the model losing functional capacity.
2. **GDN state recurrence** accumulates signals over tokens in a way that could project observational contrast into a separable channel before committing it to the residual stream.
3. **Gated attention** on 8/40 layers provides selective read/write at specific depths, plausibly supporting the causal/observational channel separation.

Dense transformers have none of these mechanisms; every direction in the residual is potentially load-bearing.

This finding is the **strongest positive** in the paper's intervention sweep: **amnesic is a hybrid-architecture signature**, not a generic reasoning booster. It distinguishes architectures in a way that no prior observational probe metric does.

**Caveats**:
- Single dense model at each scale — replication in Llama-3.3-70B / DeepSeek-V3 still open.
- N_test=44 on Qwen2.5-32B gives variance ~3 pp; direction is consistent across all 3 layers, but tighter bounds require N≥200.
- Probes on dense have low test accuracy (0.13-0.16) but above chance (0.10) — they encode some signal, which amnesic damages.

### 3.15 Architecture isolation — is it MoE, GDN, or Gated-Attn? ⭐⭐⭐

To isolate which component of the hybrid architecture is responsible for the amnesic-positive effect, we replicate the protocol on **Mixtral-8x7B-Instruct-v0.1** — a **MoE-only** model (8 experts, 2 active per token) with **standard GQA attention** and **standard MLP**. No GDN. No Gated-Attention. Clean isolation of MoE routing as the sole hybrid-distinguishing feature.

N_test = 40 (baseline 32.5%, passes ≥30% sanity threshold):

| Layer (depth%) | Acc | Δ | Gained | Lost |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| L10 (33%) | 30.0% | **−2.5 pp** | 0 | 1 |
| L16 (50%) | 27.5% | **−5.0 pp** | 0 | 2 |
| L21 (67%) | 32.5% | **0.0 pp** | 1 | 1 |

**Mixtral behaves like dense**, not like Qwen3.6 hybrid. Zero gains across the two negative layers, 1-2 losses each; even the "neutral" L21 shows 1-gained / 1-lost (not Pareto). No positive amnesic signature.

**Three-model architecture comparison**:

| Model | Arch features | Baseline | Best amnesic Δ (Pareto) |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|
| Qwen3.6-35B-A3B | **fine-grained MoE** (128/8) + **GDN** + **Gated-Attn** | 53.6% | **+2.7 pp** ✓ |
| Mixtral-8x7B | **coarse MoE** (8/2), dense attn, dense MLP | 32.5% | **−2.5 pp** ✗ |
| Qwen2.5-32B | dense, dense attn, dense MLP | 40.9% | **−4.5 pp** ✗ |

Mixtral-hybrid gap: **5.2 pp** (of the same direction as the hybrid-dense 7.2 pp gap).

**Conclusion — MoE routing alone is NOT sufficient**:
Standard MoE routing (Mixtral's 8-expert / 2-active configuration) does not enable amnesic; it behaves like a dense transformer. The Qwen3.6-specific effect must come from one of:
1. **GDN state recurrence** (32 of 40 layers) — accumulates signals in a way that may project observational contrast into a separable channel
2. **Gated Attention** (8 of 40 layers) — enables selective read/write at specific depths
3. **Fine-grained MoE routing** (128 experts, 8 active per token) — creates representational redundancy absent from coarse 8-expert MoE
4. Some combination of (1)-(3)

Standard (coarse) MoE routing is **ruled out as the sole enabler**. This is a specific mechanistic constraint: the amnesic phenomenon in Qwen3.6 reflects its State-Space (GDN), Gated-Attention, or fine-grained-routing structure — not MoE as a generic paradigm.

**Future isolation experiments** (left for follow-up work):
- **DeepSeek-V2-Lite** (fine-grained MoE, 64/6, standard attn) — isolates fine-grained-routing contribution
- **Pure Mamba/Mamba-2 models** at matched scale — isolates SSM/GDN contribution
- **Ablation of GDN layers via substitution with dense attention** — direct causal test

## 4. Synthesized picture

The eight intervention levels converge on a single coherent mechanism:

| Intervention | Single-point manipulation | Systemic perturbation |
|--------------|:-------------------------:|:---------------------:|
| Probe patch | ❌ null | — |
| Probe ablation (teacher-forced) | ⚠️ +15pp anti-causal on source-kept | — |
| SAE feature patch | ❌ −5pp | — |
| Transcoder attribution | ❌ 0pp exact | — |
| Induced recurrence | — | ❌ −6 to −38pp (monotonic) |
| Loop-intolerance profile | — | ✅ localizes L15–L20 (−27pp) |
| Norm-preserving loop | — | ⚠️ +10pp partial rescue at N=2 |
| **Amnesic inference (real, N=617)** | ✅ **+0.9–3.0pp Pareto positive (p=0.002)** | — |
| **Cross-arch amnesic (Qwen2.5-32B dense)** | ❌ **−4.5pp (0 gains, 2 losses)** — architecture gap 7.2pp | — |
| **Architecture isolation (Mixtral-8x7B MoE-only)** | ❌ **−2.5pp (0 gains, 1-2 losses)** — MoE alone ≠ enabler | — |

**Coherent story**:
1. Reasoning in Qwen3.6-35B-A3B lives in a **distributed representation across L15–L20**.
2. That representation is **load-bearing** (destruction via loop in that range costs −27pp) but **not manipulable via single-direction interventions** (six single-point methods all null or anti-causal).
3. The L17 probe AUROC 0.78 is an **observational readout of inter-class contrast**, not a causal driver — confirmed by amnesic-probing test (ablation +15pp source-kept, §3.5) and validated on accuracy (+0.9–3.0pp Pareto-positive, 10 gained / 0 lost across 617 items, pooled p=0.002; §3.11).
4. The architecture is **not recurrently loopable without retraining** — magnitude dilution explains ~10pp of the damage (partially rescuable via norm-preservation), but compound sequential perturbation dominates.
5. **Amnesic inference is the first practical accuracy improvement**: removing the probe direction at the last prompt token yields a small, one-directional, never-negative accuracy gain — validating Elazar et al.'s "observational probes carry confidence-saturating noise" hypothesis at the reasoning level.
6. **Amnesic is a hybrid-architecture signature** (§3.14): on Qwen2.5-32B dense, the identical operation yields **−4.5 pp with 0 gains / 2 losses** — a 7.2 pp architecture gap. The probe direction is **observational in hybrid, load-bearing in dense**. MoE + GDN + Gated-Attention creates readout channels separable from write channels; dense transformers have no such separation.
7. **MoE routing alone is NOT sufficient** (§3.15): Mixtral-8x7B (MoE-only, no GDN/Gated-Attn) shows **−2.5 pp**, behaving like dense. This rules out standard MoE as the mechanism — the enabler must be **GDN state recurrence**, **Gated-Attention**, **fine-grained MoE routing** (128/8 vs Mixtral's 8/2), or their combination.

**Implications**:
- Cross-Layer Transcoders (CLT) + attribution graphs are necessary for any causal claim in hybrid MoE
- Parcae-style training (spectral-radius-constrained input injection) is necessary to make Qwen3.6-class architectures benefit from inference-time recurrence
- Loop-Intolerance Profiling is a **general method for localizing load-bearing reasoning zones** in any architecture, complementary to activation patching (which measures single-point causality) and SAE-feature analysis (which measures feature-level decomposition)

### 5.3 Loop-Intolerance Profiling (reproduce §3.9, ~1 min per range)

```python
class LoopHookManager:
    def __init__(self, layers, start, end, intercept):
        self.layers, self.start, self.end, self.intercept = layers, start, end, intercept
        self.n_loops, self.captured_kwargs, self.applied = 0, None, False
    def _capture(self, module, args, kwargs):
        if self.captured_kwargs is None:
            self.captured_kwargs = {k: v for k, v in kwargs.items() if k != 'past_key_values'}
    def _intercept(self, module, args, kwargs):
        if self.n_loops == 0 or self.applied or self.captured_kwargs is None: return None
        hidden = args[0]
        safe = {k: v for k, v in self.captured_kwargs.items() if k != 'past_key_values'}
        for _ in range(self.n_loops):
            for i in range(self.start, self.end + 1):
                out = self.layers[i](hidden, **safe)
                hidden = out[0] if isinstance(out, tuple) else out
        self.applied = True
        return (hidden,) + args[1:], kwargs
    def install(self, layers):
        self.h1 = layers[self.start].register_forward_pre_hook(self._capture, with_kwargs=True)
        self.h2 = layers[self.intercept].register_forward_pre_hook(self._intercept, with_kwargs=True)
```

## 6. Limitations and Honesty Notes

1. **N = 20–50 per intervention**. Paired-bootstrap CIs for ±5 pp effects span `[−10pp, +15pp]` — effects below 10 pp are within the noise floor. A follow-up with N ≥ 200 is planned.

2. **All interventions are single-layer or single-range.** Full CLT + multi-layer attribution graphs are the next step; our results are a *lower bound on causal manipulability*, not a proof of non-manipulability under richer methods.

3. **SAE fidelity 56% / transcoder 55%**. The missing 44–45% of variance might contain the causal signal. Larger dictionaries (d_sae = 32 k, k = 128) may change §3.6 and §3.7.

4. **SuperGPQA MCQ is a narrow domain.** Anthropic's original planning finding was on creative writing. Our negative results may not generalize.

5. **GDN layers (32/40) are excluded** from intervention. They carry recurrent state we cannot point-intervene without custom SSM-state surgery.

6. **"Loop-Intolerance Profiling"** is our first empirical proposal — validation on dense transformers, other MoE models, and code/math reasoning tasks is ongoing.
